About Us

We are a team of bioinformatics students dedicated to improving the early detection of schizophrenia. Our goal is to use advanced machine learning models to analyze brain imaging and brain signals(EEG signals) data to identify individuals who may be at risk for developing the disease.

We aim to get reliable diagnoses of schizophrenia so With the help of our scientists we hope in the future we can save so many people before losing their social life and their life to Schizophrenia .



Our vision

A platform capable of providing a reliable diagnoses of schizophrenia.

Our mission

Applying deep learning techniques to brain signals for a better understanding of the disorder and to develop more accurate diagnoses of Schizophrenia.



The motivation

Due to the the spread of this disease around the world and the lack of quality in diagnosing this disease using the usual computer methods that we had to to provide a more accurate and objective diagnosis of the disorder so that early detection and intervention are vital for better prognosis.



How we did it ?

Data of 81 subjects used in the experiment were measured to study the difference in EEG between schizophrenic patients and healthy subjects. N100 refers to a negative deflection in EEG brain waves after 100 ms when auditory stimulation is given. Schizophrenia patients have problems with N100 because they have hearing impairment due to a dysfunction in the auditory cortex. Thus, a study was conducted to verify the difference in N100 between schizophrenic and healthy patients.

EEG was measured in a total of 70 channels by measuring 64-channel scalp EEG and 6 channels around the eyes and nose. In this study that compared the N100 differences between schizophrenic patients and healthy subjects , among the 70 analyzed channels,only 9 electrode sites had distinct N100 , by training our models on this data we were able to get reliable accuracy.

Our models


We applied deep learning techniques to EEG signals for a better understanding of the disorder and to develop more accurate diagnoses of Schizophrenia so in this project, we have trained, tested, and evaluated LSTM and CNN models that aim at detecting Schizophrenia with an accuracy up to 98% on both models.



How to use the platform?



The models were built to receive the input data the EEG signals in the form of CSV format to proceed with the preproccessing and then into the model.

That's why our users have to convert the raw EEG signals to a CSV format so that it will be suitable for the platform.
